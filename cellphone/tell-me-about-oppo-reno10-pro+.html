VoIP stands for Voice over Internet Protocol. In plain English, it means sending your voice over the internet as digital data packets, rather than sending it as analog electrical signals over a traditional phone line or radio waves to a cell tower.

Free Wi-Fi Calling is simply using a VoIP app on a device connected to Wi-Fi to make phone calls and send texts without needing a cellular service plan or a SIM card. Because your clone phone has a working Wi-Fi chip, it can use these technologies to act exactly like a fully functional home phone.

Here is a breakdown of how it works and the best free options to get that phone running over your home Wi-Fi grid.

How Free Wi-Fi Calling Apps Work

When you download a Wi-Fi calling app, the provider gives you a virtual phone number (usually with a local Las Vegas area code like 702 or 725 if you choose).

  • Outbound: When you dial a number, the app converts your voice into data, routes it over your Wi-Fi, and drops it into the regular phone network. The person on the other end sees your virtual number on their caller ID.
  • Inbound: When someone calls your assigned virtual number, the app rings on your phone over Wi-Fi, exactly like a regular phone call.

The Best Free Apps to Fix Your Phone

Since your phone runs Android, you can open the Google Play Store over Wi-Fi and install these options for free:

1. Google Voice (Best Overall)

Google Voice is entirely free, completely ad-free, and highly reliable. It gives you a real, permanent US phone number.

  • What you get: Unlimited free calling and texting to the US and Canada. It also transcribes your voicemails into text.
  • The Catch: To activate a Google Voice number the very first time, Google requires you to link it to an existing, valid US phone number (like a friend's cell or a home landline) just to verify you are a real person. Once verified, you can unlink it, and the app runs entirely standalone over Wi-Fi.

Very similar to TextNow. It gives you a free local phone number and unlimited inbound and outbound US/Canada calls and SMS over Wi-Fi. It is also ad-supported, making it an excellent burner or backup line.
