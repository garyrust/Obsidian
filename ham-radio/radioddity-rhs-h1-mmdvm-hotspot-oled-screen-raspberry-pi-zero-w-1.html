Simplify your setup with Radioddity's RHS-H1 MMDVM Hotspot. Equipped with preloaded Pi-Star software on the TF card, it delivers a true plug-and-play experience—just power it up and connect, with no additional downloads or configuration needed. This hotspot supports multiple digital modes, including DMR, D-STAR, YSF, NXDN, and P25, allowing for seamless protocol switching and flexible communication with digital radios. Ideal for expanding your reach, the RHS-H1 allows you to connect to these networks via the internet, bypassing the need for a physical repeater and enhancing flexibility in communication.

Instructions:
Visit http://www.pistar.uk/wifi\_builder.php and enter your home Wi-Fi SSID and password (PSK).
Download the wpa_supplicant.conf file, which includes your network details.
Save this file to the root directory of your TF card (e.g., F:).
Insert the TF card and power on the device. After 2–3 minutes, it will automatically connect to your home Wi-Fi.
To verify the connection, check your router for the Pi-Star device and note its IP address.

To verify the connection, you can try pinging pi-star from your computer. If successful, open http://pi-star or enter the Pi-Star’s IP address in your browser. Log in with the default credentials (username: pi-star, password: raspberry). Once logged in, you can configure your settings, including CALLSIGN, ID, Frequency, and Modem.

This is a good device that does nearly every necessary protocol for digital ham radio. It worked right out of the box. However, as others have noted, the instructions that come with it are nearly worthless. There are better instructions, but you have to ask Radioddity Support for them. The particular parts used to build this hotspot are not set forth by Radioddity and this is information that may be necessary as time passes and new versions of software are released. My particular gripe with the whole experience is that this item is sold with software (Ver 3.1.17) that was released over 6 years ago. It ships with an equally old OS. There are bugs in Ver 3.1.17 that may prevent the user (it did me) from updating anything, even the OS (which is the only thing updatable). The software author identified the problem as a software bug and he posted a workaround online and promised to fix the issue in a later version (which appears to have been in Version 4.0.0-RC2). All of the Radioddity documentation is for Ver 3.1.17. The current version of Pi-Star is 4.2.1 and there have been over a dozen updates since Ver 3.1.17. One cannot update past Ver 3.1.17 using the "Update" button. One must download the newer software from Pi-Star and burn a new image. So, if you want to do a turnkey installation, you will do it with Ver 3.1.17 and the Radioddity documentation. If you want the current version of Pi-Star, you will be downloading Pi-Star 4.x, downloading SD card burning software, and burning a new SD card. Then, your best bet for documentation will be online. Mine is working perfectly, now, but it didn't turn out to be the turnkey installation that was advertised and that I was expecting.
