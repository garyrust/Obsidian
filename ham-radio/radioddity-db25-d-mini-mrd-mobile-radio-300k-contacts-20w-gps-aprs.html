As one of the most compact DMR mobiles in the market, the Radioddity DB25-D is about the size of a handheld radio. Small but powerful would be the perfect description for this palm size mobile radio.

The DB25-D supports both digital and analog mode operation on VHF/UHF frequencies. It features 20W high power output, 500k DMR contacts storage and 7 customizable function keys. What’s more, DB25-D comes with GPS function, supporting APRS, being your ideal choice for off-road and outdoor use.

APRS enables your radio to send and receive packets of GEO-information include text messages, weather information, and GPS data. Your friends can track you easily when you’re traveling in the backcountry. CLICK HERE to learn about how to set up APRS. The Radioddity DB25-D comes with a microphone option, which can be set according to different situations in order to improve your TX audio quality. Knowing the fact that more and more people are migrating to DMR operation, the DB25-D supports larger digital contacts storage while providing 198 hours of audio recording and with its dual mode capability, making migration even easier. The DB25D has a total of 7 customizable function keys that can activate the preset functions. Each key has Long / Short press option, making up a total of 14 different functions being available with a single keypress.

I have had this radio for 2 years now and it is well worth the money. I used it on DMR and analog, great audio. I like the 20W output and it takes a baofeng H-250 handset for use on my Ebike, I mounted it a few moths ago and use it on my ebaik. Great fun, now I need another one in the hamshack. An update to my previous review. After returning my original (open box) faulty radio, I received a new unit. I was pleased that this one did not immediately go up in smoke when powered on. In analog mode, this radio works adequately. Transmit and receive audio are good, no complaints. In digital mode, things get remarkably worse. Firstly, the radio is very selective about what DMR repeaters it will talk to at all. My radio does not work with my local repeater, EXCEPT when located directly under the repeater, using a dummy load. It will not talk to the repeater using any actual ante nna, although these worked fine on every other DMR radio we tested, as did the repeater. Get 20 feet away from the repeater, and the radio won't talk to it whatsoever. It DOES work on every hotspot we have tried. I had to purchase a MMDVM hotspot to make my radio operable. All my settings for the repeater were correct, and confirmed by the owner of the repeater--it just doesn't work. The microphone is VERY quiet on digital, but fine on analog. There is no option to change the gain for one mode and not the other. Similarly, speaker volume is normal in analog, and ALL OVER THE PLACE in digital, ranging from a whisper to ear-splittingly loud. This could be a quirk of DMR, and not the radio, however. An analog volume control knob would be VERY helpful. It's not a good sign when I wish this radio has features from my Baofeng. The CPS is awful; you already know. Downloading the 3rd party CPS makes the process tolerable, but not great. I can find no explanation for the difference between contacts, ham contacts, and ham groups. I've programmed the radio, and still don't know the difference. Long story short, this is my second DB25-D, and I have gotten this one to work, but barely, with great difficulty, and by purchasing extra equipment. I'm keeping mine, but it was a close decision. I would recommend that others stay away.
